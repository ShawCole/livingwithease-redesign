Design Direction

This is an art-directed, narrative-driven redesign — not a template swap. Every page will be individually designed with intentional typography, atmospheric imagery, and a visual story that matches the depth of Jayne's transformational work.

  • Distinctive font pairing: script/cursive for warmth + clean sans-serif for clarity
  • Full-bleed imagery with authentic photography as the emotional core
  • Artistic elements woven throughout — textures, icons, thematic motifs
  • Restrained color story (6-8 colors) with warm, earthy neutrals
  • Narrative flow — storytelling copy that guides visitors through a journey
  • One page art-directed first, then duplicated for consistency

Theme Motif & Textures

Every strong brand has a visual thread that runs through it. Based on Jayne's work — nervous system recalibration and a return to your most authentic self — we're proposing a core motif and supporting texture language, filtered through an editorial luxury lens.

Proposed Motif: The Unfurling Fern
A fiddlehead fern slowly unfurling — representing the gradual, organic process of transformation. Not a butterfly (instant metamorphosis) but a fern: growth that happens from the inside out, one layer at a time. Rooted. Earthy. Patient. Can be rendered as line art, watercolor, or photographic element woven throughout the site.
Alternative: Concentric Rings
Tree rings or water ripples radiating outward — representing the Three Pillars (Energy Health, Alignment, Blueprint) as concentric layers. Geometric enough for modern design, organic enough for the earthy brand. Could serve as section dividers, background textures, or icon framework.
Texture: Linen / Natural Paper
Subtle linen or handmade paper texture overlaid on background sections. Adds warmth and tactility without competing with content. Think: the feeling of holding a high-quality journal.
Texture: Soft Watercolor Washes
Sage and gold watercolor washes as section backgrounds or card accents. Hand-painted feeling that reinforces the human, artisanal quality of Jayne's work. Not digital gradients — organic, imperfect edges.

Imagery

Photography Direction

The right photography is the emotional core of the site. Stock imagery won't cut it for this kind of work. Here's what we recommend for a brand like Jayne's.

Professional Headshots
2-3 high-quality portraits of Jayne in different settings. Warm, approachable, confident. Not corporate-stiff — think: natural light, genuine expression, shot in a space that feels like her (home office, garden, studio).
Used for: Hero section, About page, Contact page, testimonials sidebar
Lifestyle / In-Session
Candid or semi-candid shots of Jayne in her element. Working with a client (with permission), hands in motion, deep conversation. These images communicate the intimacy and care of the work without words.
Used for: Services page, About page sections, social proof
Atmospheric / Nature
Carefully curated nature imagery that reinforces the brand palette: ferns unfurling, morning light through trees, still water, stone textures. Not generic stock — images that feel intentionally chosen and color-graded to match the sage/gold/cream palette.
Used for: Section backgrounds, page heroes, texture overlays
Detail / Texture Shots
Close-up details that add richness: hands on a journal, a candle flame, crystals on linen, sage leaves, dried botanicals. These fill the spaces between major content sections and create the tactile, hand-crafted feeling.
Used for: Dividers, card backgrounds, mobile header images
